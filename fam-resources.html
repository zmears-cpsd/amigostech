💻 Student Device Use at Amigos

This section explains how student devices are used at Amigos, how access is managed, and what students can expect when using technology at school.

Devices and Access

Students at Amigos use Chromebooks as part of their learning, to support academic work and projects.

Device access varies by grade level:

  • Pre-K and K: Usage varies by classroom.
  • Grade 1: The two classrooms share a single set of chromebooks.
  • Grades 2–4: Each student has a chromebook assigned for use at school.
  • Grades 5–8: Students are responsible for managing their own device throughout the school day.
  • Grades 6–8 (and many 5th grade students): Devices are taken home daily.

Students in grades 1–4 who do not have access to a device at home may be eligible for a take-home Chromebook. Families can contact their child’s teacher for more information.

Accounts and Login

School-issued Chromebooks are designed to work only with district-managed student accounts.

  • Students must use their CPS account to log in. Personal accounts cannot be used on school devices.
  • All student work is completed and saved within district-managed platforms.

This helps ensure that student work is secure and that all tools used in school meet district safety and privacy standards.

Internet Access and Filtering

Student chromebooks are connected to a filtered internet system at school, and have on-device filters to support safety outside of school. Only school-appropriate content is accessible on student devices.

While these systems are designed to be effective, no filtering system is perfect. Students are expected to use devices responsibly and report anything inappropriate to a teacher.

Monitoring and Classroom Use

Technology use at school is structured and supervised. Devices are learning tools, not for personal or entertainment use unless directed by a teacher.

  • Teachers actively monitor student device use during class
  • Teachers may use tools such as GoGuardian to view and guide student activity
  • Students primarily use school-approved platforms such as Google Classroom, Docs, Slides, and otherGoogle for Education tools

Devices are used to support instruction, collaboration, and creative work as part of the curriculum.

🏠 Supporting Safe Tech Use at Home
Balancing Screen Time

Encourage a balance between screen-based activities and offline play, reading, and family time.

Encouraging Creation

Whenever possible, guide children toward creating (drawing, building, designing) rather than only consuming content.

Talking About Online Behavior

Conversations about kindness, respect, and responsibility online can help children make thoughtful choices when using technology.

📜 School and District Policies

This section provides a simplified overview of how school technology is used and what families should know about expectations, privacy, and required forms.

Student Chromebook Use

1) School devices, accounts, and internet access are provided to support learning. They are owned and managed by the district. This means:

  • The device belongs to the school
  • The student account is managed by the district
  • Use of school devices and accounts should not be considered private

2) Activity on school devices and accounts is actively monitored while at school. Usage and data may be reviewed if needed to support student safety and ensure appropriate use.

3) Students are expected to use school technology in a responsible, respectful, and school-appropriate way. This includes:

  • Using devices for learning and school-related activities
  • Keeping personal information safe
  • Being respectful and appropriate when communicating online
  • Following teacher and school guidelines for technology use

This is a simplified overview provided for convenience purposes only. It does not replace the district's official policy. Families should review the full Acceptable Use Policy for complete details:

View the full Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

At-Home Hotspot Use

Families without internet access at home can receive a hotspot from the district to support learning at home. These devices are provided to help students access schoolwork and academic resources.

Please note:

  • The hotspot is designed to work only with school-issued Chromebooks. It should not be used with personal phones, tablets, or other devices.
  • It is intended for schoolwork and educational use only.
  • It should be used during the school year and returned at the end of the year, unless the family has district approval to use the device for summer learning needs.

While using a hotspot, the district can and does monitor how much data is being used, but cannot see specific browsing activity. Devices remain filtered, however, and browsing history is still saved on the device.

If a hotspot is not being utilized, or is being used inappropriately, the school may request that it be returned.

Privacy and Data

Student devices, accounts, and internet access are managed by the school district to support learning and safety.

This means:

  • Activity on school devices and accounts is actively monitored while at school
  • Use of school-managed technology should not be considered private
  • Usage and data may be reviewed if needed to support safety or investigate concerns

Additionally, the district takes student data & privacy seriously. Student information is protected under district policy and state & federal law. As such, it is crucial that students use only their own accounts and only use their accounts with approved tools and platforms.

Forms and Permissions

At the beginning of each year, families are asked to review and complete a number of important school forms through the CPSD Family Portal.

These forms include various district and school forms. The most important for technology usage purposes are:

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
  • Student Device Agreement
  • Media Release (permission to share student's image, name, or work with our CPS community)

Completing these forms allows students to use school technology and participate fully in class activities.

If you need help accessing or completing these forms, please contact the school for support.

🛠️ Help & FAQs
Common Questions

Why can’t my child access certain websites? Why does Google Classroom sometimes appear empty? These are common issues and are usually related to filters or assignment timing.

Login Help

Make sure students are logged into their school account (not a personal account) when accessing school tools like Google Classroom.

When Something Isn’t Working

Try refreshing the page, logging out and back in, or restarting the device. If problems continue, reach out for support.
